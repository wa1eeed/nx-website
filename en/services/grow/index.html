The symptoms

You already know if you have tech debt

These are the signals we hear before every NX Grow engagement. If two or more sound familiar, the interest is already compounding.

Shipping keeps slowing

Features that used to take days now take weeks. Every change risks breaking something unrelated.

Fragile in production

Recurring incidents, mystery bugs, and a deploy process the whole team holds its breath through.

Buckles under load

The platform slows or falls over during peak traffic, exactly when it matters most commercially.

No tests, no safety net

Little or no automated coverage, so every release is a leap of faith and every refactor is feared.

Compliance gaps

Security and regulatory requirements were never built in, and now they're blocking a deal or a license.

Key-man risk

One or two people understand the system, nothing is documented, and onboarding anyone new takes months.

Common questions

NX Grow, answered

Will you have to rewrite everything from scratch?

Almost never. Big-bang rewrites are high-risk and usually unnecessary. We modernize incrementally, refactoring and re-architecting the highest-impact areas first, behind feature flags, so the business keeps running throughout.

Our code was largely AI-generated. Is it salvageable?

Usually yes. AI-generated code tends to work in the happy path but lacks tests, security hardening, and structural coherence. The audit tells us exactly what to keep, what to refactor, and what to replace, without throwing away working functionality.

How disruptive is this to our current team?

Minimal. We work alongside your team, not over them, and a core goal is leaving them with documentation, tests and runbooks so they own a faster, cleaner platform afterward. Many clients report their own velocity improving during the engagement.

How long does an engagement take?

It depends on the size and state of the codebase, which the audit determines precisely. Most engagements run 8-20 weeks, with value shipping continuously throughout rather than landing all at the end.
