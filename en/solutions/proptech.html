Where proptech platforms fail

Demand exposes everything

The patterns that break Saudi real-estate platforms once volume arrives — and the engineering choices that prevent them.

Double-bookings

Two buyers reserve the same unit; whoever shouts loudest wins. Manual reconciliation drains the agent team and breaks customer trust.

Deed mismatches

Listing data drifts from the Ministry of Justice deed registry. Buyers walk away mid-process when ownership doesn't reconcile.

Ejar contract chaos

Manual contract entry into Ejar, errors caught after signing, tenant frustration. The flow needs to be native, not bolted on later.

GIS performance

Map renders crawl with 50k+ listings. Filter changes block the UI; users assume the platform is broken and bounce.

Escrow gaps

Deposit handling without proper escrow flow — funds bounce between accounts, audit trails are absent, regulator concerns mount.

Broker-data silos

Each broker maintains their own listing spreadsheet. The platform shows stale prices and pulled units to real customers, daily.

Common questions

Engineering Saudi proptech, answered

Can you integrate directly with REGA and Ejar?

Yes — that's a core NX Connect capability. We integrate Ejar contract creation, REGA transactional mapping and deed verification flows into your platform, with audit trails the regulator expects.

What if our current platform already has data inconsistencies?

That's exactly what an NX Grow engagement addresses — we sequence the migration so the data model stabilizes first, then layer the new bidding and contract flows on top, keeping the business live throughout.

Will the platform handle a National Day-level surge?

It will, because we load-test it specifically against those scenarios. The bidding engine is built event-sourced and elastic, so concurrency is bounded by design rather than by hoping the spike doesn't happen.

Do you support off-plan / pre-sale flows?

Yes. Off-plan reservation, milestone-based payments, escrow and conversion to Ejar at handover are all standard patterns in our proptech engagements.
